### For Students

#### Scenario 1: Suspicious Email
**Situation**: Receive an email claiming to be from your school asking you to "verify your account" by clicking a link.

**Action**:
1. Copy the email content
2. Paste it into the "Email & Messages" tab
3. Let the tool analyze it
4. If HIGH or CRITICAL risk: Don't click anything, delete the email
5. Contact your school's IT support directly

**Expected Result**: Tool identifies phishing attempt

---

#### Scenario 2: Shortened URL in Chat
**Situation**: Friend sends you a link shortened with bit.ly from an unknown source.

**Action**:
1. Copy the link (don't click it!)
2. Paste it into the "Links" tab
3. Select "Messaging App" as context
4. Review the analysis
5. Ask your friend where they got it or verify it's legitimate before clicking

**Expected Result**: Tool flags suspicious characteristics

---

#### Scenario 3: Unsolicited Prize Offer
**Situation**: Email says you've won a prize and need to "claim it now" by confirming personal information.

**Action**:
1. Analyze the email content
2. Check for social engineering tactics
3. Remember: If you didn't enter a contest, you didn't win
4. Delete without clicking anything

**Expected Result**: Tool identifies urgency and reward tactics
